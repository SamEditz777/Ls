SL - 2025  |  Experiment 13
Title: CrypTool — Symmetric & Asymmetric Encryption + Digital Signatures

NOTE
- CrypTool primarily runs on Windows; use CT-online demos if on Ubuntu. Do not reuse demo keys for production.

PART A — AES (Symmetric)
1) Open CrypTool (or CT-online AES demo)
2) Choose AES demo; enter plaintext and a key
3) Encrypt → observe ciphertext
4) Decrypt with same key → verify plaintext
5) Flip one key bit → observe avalanche effect in ciphertext

PART B — RSA (Asymmetric)
1) Open RSA demo
2) Choose two primes or have tool generate keypair (1024/2048 demo)
3) Note N, e (public) and d (private)
4) Encrypt plaintext with public key; decrypt with private
5) Verify results match

PART C — Digital Signatures
1) Hash a message (SHA-256)
2) Sign the hash with private key
3) Verify signature with public key
4) Modify message and show verification fails

RECORD
- Parameters used (p, q, N, e, d or key lengths)
- Sample ciphertexts/hashes (non-sensitive)
- Screenshots of operations

SECURITY NOTE
- Prefer RSA-2048+ and SHA-256+ for anything beyond a demo
